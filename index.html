01 // Mathematical Frameworks

We don't rely on raw formulas. We look for patterns.

The AMC 8 doesn't just test how fast you can calculate; it rewards deep problem comprehension. We show students how to break down intricate wording into simple, visual properties to isolate the correct answers cleanly.

Strategic Symmetry

Many complex counting and geometry problems contain hidden balance. By recognizing uniform structures early, you can solve half the problem and accurately scale up your results without making trivial errors.

Isolating Modular Cycles

Number theory problems frequently rely on repeating patterns. We teach students to confidently map small repeating cycles to quickly figure out huge exponential remainders under tight exam limits.

02 // Syllabus Archive

Interactive Training Modules

Explore our active lesson segments. These modules are unlocked and updated here right after each classroom run.

W01 // The Backdoor Strategy: Counting the Opposite

Combinatorics & Probability Foundations

If a problem asks you to count dozens of messy possibilities, don't do it the hard way. Instead, look at the big picture. Find the total number of options, figure out the few "forbidden" ones you don't want, and subtract them. Whenever you spot phrases like "at least one," this trick will save you a ton of time.

W02 // Lineups & Lockouts: Managing Tight Constraints

Permutations & Spatial Sequences

Arranging people or items in a row gets confusing when rules pop up. We will look at how to map out seating plans and codes using slot diagrams. You will learn the exact steps to handle picky restrictions, like when two items refuse to be next to each other or when someone must sit in a specific spot.

W03 // No Order, No Noise: Grouping Without Duplicates

Combinatorics & Overcount Filters

When you are picking a team, order does not matter—picking Sam then Alex is the exact same as picking Alex then Sam. We will learn how to easily spot when order is an illusion and how to use simple division to clean out double-counted groups. This makes handshake and committee problems a breeze.

W04 // The Overlap Map: Untangling Complex Systems

Multi-Set Visual Logic & PIE

When different groups share members, normal adding causes mistakes. We will use visual maps to break these chaotic overlapping word problems into neat, separate zones. You will learn how to handle double and triple overlaps perfectly so you never accidentally count a student or item twice.

W05 // DNA of an Integer: Prime Decomposition Hacks

Number Theory & Divisor Architecture

Stop making long, exhausting lists to find the factors of a number. We are going to break integers down into their core prime numbers to see exactly how they are built. With a quick exponent shortcut, you will instantly know how many total factors a massive number has without finding them one by one.

W06 // Clockwork Math: Cracking Modular Cycles

Advanced Remainder & Exponent Mechanics

How do you find the last digit of a giant number like $7^{2026}$ without multiplying forever? You find the heartbeat of the number. Numbers love to repeat themselves in predictable cycles when raised to powers. We will learn to catch these rhythms so you can solve massive remainder problems in seconds.

W07 // Rhythm & Divisibility: Synchronizing Chaos

LCM Frameworks & Fast Filters

When clocks, alarms, or racing cars start at different times, finding out when they line up seems chaotic. We will use Least Common Multiples to pinpoint exact match-up moments. Plus, we will learn secret shortcuts for numbers like 3, 9, and 11 to check for divisibility without long division.

W08 // Mirror Patterns: Rapid Arithmetic Summations

Symmetrical Progressions & Linear Balance

Adding up a massive string of numbers one by one is a trap designed to waste your time. We will use a beautiful balancing trick to pair up the first and last numbers of a sequence. This collapses huge lists of numbers up to 1,000 into a simple, single multiplication step that takes five seconds.

W09 // Relative Speed: Closing the Gap

Dynamic Velocity Systems & Chases

Instead of stressed-out tracking of two different moving objects at the same time, just freeze one of them in place! By looking at how fast the gap between them closes or opens, we turn confusing head-to-head races and round-trips into basic, quick distance math while avoiding sneaky speed traps.

W10 // Finding the Anchor: Invariants in Motion

Algebraic Balance & Ratio Locking

When things are changing fast—water evaporating from a pool, ingredients mixing, or groups swapping members—look for the one thing that stays exactly the same. Locking onto this hidden anchor lets you build simple, perfectly balanced equations that bypass pages of ugly algebra.

W11 // The Boxing Method: Slicing Irregular Geometry

Boundary Framing & Proportional Scaling

Don't panic when you see a strange, jagged, irregular shape on the test. If you don't know a formula for it, just draw a clean rectangle around it! We will learn how to calculate the easy outer shapes and cut them away from the main box to leave you with the exact shaded answer you need.

W12 // Game Day Mindset: The Triage Blueprint

Advanced Competitive Stress Protocols

Knowing the math doesn't matter if you run out of time or freeze up under pressure. In our final week, we learn test-taking strategy. You will practice how to sort through the hardest endgame questions, when to skip, how to make smart guesses, and how to protect your score when the clock is ticking down.
